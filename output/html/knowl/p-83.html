In the previous section we were introduced to the notion of a set, its elements, the cardinality of a finite set, the notion of subsets, and also the notion of equality between sets. In this section, we'll be introduced to four common operations on sets.

  • The complement of a set, denoted by \(A^c\) or \(A'\text{.}\)

  • The intersection between two sets, denoted by \(A\cap B\text{.}\)

  • The union between two sets, denoted by \(A\cup B\text{.}\)

  • The difference between two sets, denoted by \(A - B\text{.}\)
