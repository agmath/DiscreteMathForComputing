Section 10.1 Sample Project Guidelines

Note. These are sample project guidelines that we've provided to our students when offering this course at SNHU. We offer them here in case they are helpful.

Requirements. Pick a project from the topics described in the sections of this chapter. Your completed project should include the following.

  1. Relevant Definitions: Be sure to define all of the terms you are using, unless we have specifically addressed that term in our course.

  2. Explanation: What is the topic trying to address? If you are using formulas or algorithms, why do they work? This should be the bulk of your project.

  3. Example: Demonstrate with at least one example. You should explain each step in your example(s). Your example(s) also needs to be original. This means that you cannot use the exact same example that your sources used in their descriptions.

Format. This project could be:

  • a paper

  • a poster

  • a slide deck with video presentation

  • a website

  • for the game topics, can be a coded interactive game with written description

  • different modalities are possible, reach out to propose an idea

Logistics. Please pay attention to the following requirements.

  • Your project should be as detailed as necessary to suffiently explain your topic.

  • You must have a bibliography including at least three sources. You may use whatever citation style you want, but you MUST cite every source that you use for the project. You may use Wikipedia as one of your sources (but only one). You can also use the textbook as one of your sources if applicable.

  • You should take special care with communication in your project; your project must be understandable by someone else in the class who chose a different project topic.

Support. I am very happy to talk with you about the available project topics to help you decide which topic is best for you. I am also happy to hep you work through an example to make sure you understand your topic. We will be working on the projects for the last week of the term, so you will have opportunities to discuss the topics with me and with your classmates. As always, you are also welcome in Office Hours.
